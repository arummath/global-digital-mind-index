# 🧠 Global Digital Mind Index (GDMI)

> **A Comparative Study of AI Legal Personhood across Great Power Regulators.**

## 🌐 Overview
Inspired by the **Cambridge "Digital Minds" Initiative**, this project analyzes the transition of AI from "Tool" to "Legal Agent." We compare the legislative frameworks of the **USA, UK, EU, Russia, China, India, and Japan.**

## 🔍 Core Research Pillars
1. **Liability Models:** Who is responsible when a "Digital Mind" errs?
2. **The Personhood Gap:** Measuring the distance between cultural acceptance and legal reality.
3. **Sovereignty vs. Rights:** How national security interests shape AI morality.

## 📋  Project Roadmap
- [ ] **Phase 1:** Comparative Matrix of current AI Acts (2024-2026).
- [ ] **Phase 2:** Sentiment analysis of "Digital Rights" in public forums.
- [ ] **Phase 3:** Visualizing the "Personhood Gap" heatmap.

## 🛠 Tech & Methodology
- **Data:** Legislative PDFs, Social Media Scrapes.
- **Tools:** Python (NLP), Markdown, Comparative Jurisprudence.
